Special Ones Football Club, established in 2024, represents the pride of Islamabad, Pakistan. Founded by passionate students from the National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), this dynamic club has quickly risen through the ranks of Pakistani football. With determination and skill, our players from prestigious institutions have brought a new energy to the local football scene, combining academic excellence with athletic prowess.

The club's journey began in the heart of Islamabad, where young talents from NUST and surrounding areas came together with a shared vision. From humble beginnings training on university grounds to competing in premier tournaments across Pakistan, Special Ones FC has become synonymous with dedication, teamwork, and the relentless pursuit of excellence. Our players represent not just a club, but the dreams and aspirations of Pakistan's youth.


In our debut season, Special Ones FC made an incredible impact in the Pakistan Premier League, finishing in the top 5 despite being newcomers. Our star midfielder Ahmed Raza from F-10 Islamabad scored 12 goals, while captain Hassan Ali from G-9 led the defense with unwavering determination. The team's tactical brilliance under coach Tariq Ahmed from Blue Area has been the talk of Islamabad football circles.

Key victories against established clubs like Karachi Kings and Lahore Lions have put Special Ones on the map. Our memorable 3-1 victory against Peshawar United at the Pakistan Sports Complex, Islamabad, with goals from Faisal Khan (F-7), Muhammad Usman (I-8), and Bilal Sheikh (G-11), remains one of the season's highlights. The club's attacking style and never-give-up attitude have won hearts across the twin cities of Islamabad and Rawalpindi.


Special Ones FC has participated in multiple prestigious tournaments across Pakistan. Our breakthrough came at the Islamabad Cup 2024, where we defeated teams from all major sectors including representatives from F-6, F-8, G-10, and I-9. The finals against Rawalpindi Warriors ended 2-0 in our favor, with spectacular goals from striker Kamran Malik from DHA Phase 2 and winger Shahzad Ahmed from Bahria Town.

The Margalla Hills Trophy saw another impressive run, reaching the semi-finals after victories against clubs from Wah Cantt, Taxila, and Gujar Khan. Our goalkeeper Zain Abbas from Sector H-13 made crucial saves throughout the tournament. The National University Championship remains our crown jewel, where our NUST connection gave us home advantage, and players like Asad Raza from PWD and Junaid Khan from CDA made the university proud.


Looking ahead, Special Ones FC is set to expand its academy programs across Islamabad's sectors. Our youth development initiative, led by former Pakistan international Rashid Ahmed from Blue Area, aims to scout talent from areas like E-11, F-11, G-13, and beyond. The club's vision extends to representing Pakistan in international tournaments and producing players for the national team.

With support from the local community in areas like Gulberg, Model Town, and University Town, Special Ones FC continues to grow. Our training facilities near NUST and partnerships with local schools in I-10, I-14, and surrounding areas ensure a steady pipeline of talent. The dream is to see Special Ones FC players wearing the green jersey of Pakistan, carrying forward the legacy that began in the classrooms and football fields of Islamabad.
